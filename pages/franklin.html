Local Knowledge

Why Franklin Homeowners Choose Cornerstone

Franklin is one of the most architecturally thoughtful communities in the Nashville metro, and that standard extends to concrete work. We've completed projects in Westhaven, Fieldstone Farms, Ladd Park, and throughout Franklin's older residential areas near downtown. In each context, we bring designs and materials that fit the neighborhood's aesthetic — matching the warm limestone and brick tones common in Franklin's historic character areas, or the clean contemporary lines preferred in newer master-planned developments.

Franklin's clay-heavy soil is a particular challenge we're experienced with. The Williamson County clay band that runs through much of Franklin expands significantly when wet and shrinks during dry periods — causing more ground movement than most homeowners realize. We build our Franklin driveway and patio installations with this in mind: proper depth excavation, adequate base material, and control joint placement that accommodates the minor seasonal movement without allowing it to crack the surface in visible locations.

Franklin's newer developments also bring a unique challenge: post-construction soil compaction. In subdivisions where grading was completed by developers, the soil adjacent to homes often needs additional compaction time before concrete work begins. We assess soil stability during our Franklin estimate visits and sometimes recommend a short waiting period or enhanced base preparation — which is always less expensive than repairing concrete that settled because the underlying soil wasn't ready.

Questions

Franklin Concrete FAQ

Does Franklin, TN require permits for driveway work?+
The City of Franklin has specific permit requirements. Most residential driveway replacements on private property don't require permits, but work that changes the curb cut or affects the public right-of-way does. We verify all requirements for your specific address as part of the estimate process.
How does Franklin's clay soil affect concrete installation?+
Franklin's clay-heavy soil is more expansive than most homeowners realize — it moves seasonally, which puts stress on concrete flatwork. We address this with proper excavation depth, adequate compacted aggregate base, and strategic control joint placement. This is a significant part of why we insist on site visits before quoting.
Can you work in new Franklin subdivisions where landscaping isn't complete?+
Yes — we work in new construction environments regularly throughout Franklin. Access, material staging, and site conditions in active construction areas require extra coordination. We assess these factors during the estimate and build them into our project plan. For post-construction soil that hasn't fully compacted, we sometimes recommend enhanced base preparation.
