What's Included in Every Epoxy Floor Installation

A proper epoxy garage floor Nashville installation is a multi-day, multi-step process. Here's what you get with every Cornerstone epoxy project — no shortcuts, no acid-wash shortcuts.

✓

Diamond Grinding Surface Prep

We grind the concrete surface with industrial diamond tooling — not acid washing, which leaves salts behind and compromises adhesion. Proper profile means the coating bonds permanently, not temporarily.

✓

Moisture Testing

Concrete moisture content is tested before any coating is applied. Moisture vapor transmission that exceeds epoxy manufacturer specs will cause delamination — we won't coat a slab that isn't ready.

✓

Base Coat Application

High-solids epoxy base coat applied at proper mil thickness, ensuring the coating layer has the build needed to provide genuine protection and longevity — not the thin coats common in DIY kits.

✓

Broadcast Flake System

Color flake is broadcast into the wet base coat at full saturation for a seamless, professional appearance. We carry dozens of flake blends and sizes — you choose what fits your style.

✓

UV-Stable Polyaspartic Top Coat

Our top coat is a UV-stable polyaspartic, not a standard epoxy clear — it won't yellow or amber over time from UV exposure, keeping your garage floor looking fresh for years.

✓

Anti-Slip Finish

We broadcast a fine anti-slip aggregate into the top coat in wet areas and entry points — garage floors can get wet, and we make sure yours stays safe when it does.

How We Work

Our Epoxy Floor Installation Process

Properly installed epoxy takes two days minimum. Here's exactly what happens on a Cornerstone epoxy garage floor project in Nashville.

1

Surface Prep

Day 1: Diamond grinding, crack and joint filling, moisture testing. No coating touches the floor until the surface is properly profiled and verified dry.

2

Prime & Base Coat

High-solids epoxy primer followed by the color base coat at proper wet mil thickness. Flake broadcast immediately into the wet coat.

3

Flake & Scrape

Base coat cures overnight. Excess flake scraped off, surface lightly sanded smooth. Any touch-ups or repairs completed at this stage.

4

Top Coat & Cure

Day 2: UV-stable polyaspartic top coat applied. Light foot traffic in 24 hours. Vehicle traffic after 72 hours. Full cure at 7 days.

Why Cornerstone

Why Choose Cornerstone for Your Epoxy Garage Floor in Nashville

Surface Preparation Is Everything — and Most Contractors Skip It

The single most common cause of epoxy floor failure is inadequate surface preparation. Acid washing — the prep method used by most DIY kits and many budget contractors — opens the surface pores but leaves calcium salts behind that inhibit bonding. These floors look perfect for 6–18 months, then begin to delaminate in sheets. We use industrial diamond grinding equipment that mechanically profiles the concrete surface, removing contaminants and creating the CSP (Concrete Surface Profile) that professional coating systems require for permanent adhesion. It's louder and more labor-intensive than acid washing, but it's the only prep method that produces a coating with genuine longevity.

Cure Time Is Not Optional

Nashville's climate — particularly humidity levels in summer — affects epoxy cure rates significantly. We monitor conditions before and during application, and we won't apply coating in temperatures or humidity levels outside manufacturer specifications. We also follow proper inter-coat timing — each layer needs adequate cure time before the next is applied. Rushing the process produces a floor that looks done but hasn't fully crosslinked, leading to soft spots, delamination, or a top coat that peels away from the base. When Cornerstone schedules an epoxy garage floor Nashville installation, the timeline is built around proper cure — not around getting off the job as fast as possible.

The Right Flake and Top Coat for Nashville's Heat

Standard epoxy top coats yellow and amber under UV exposure — this is a well-known characteristic of aromatic epoxy systems. Nashville garages see significant sunlight exposure, especially in south-facing properties. We use a UV-stable polyaspartic aliphatic top coat as standard, which maintains its clarity and color indefinitely under UV exposure. We also carry a full library of flake blends and sizes so your garage floor reflects your style — from neutral grays to bold charcoal and gold blends, from fine flake for a refined look to full-chip for maximum texture. The result is an epoxy garage floor Nashville homeowners are genuinely proud of for years, not just weeks.

Questions

Epoxy Flooring FAQ

How long does professionally installed epoxy flooring last?+
A professionally installed, diamond-ground epoxy floor with proper top coat typically lasts 15–20 years in a residential garage with normal use. Commercial applications with heavy forklift traffic may need re-coating sooner. The key factors are surface prep quality, coating mil thickness, and top coat type. Our installations come with a written warranty — ask us for specifics at estimate time.
Can epoxy be applied over an existing epoxy floor?+
Sometimes, but it depends on the condition and adhesion of the existing coating. We assess the existing system during your free estimate — if it's still properly bonded, we can prep and recoat. If it's delaminating or has compromised adhesion, it needs to be ground off completely before a new system is applied. We'll give you an honest assessment.
How long before I can park my car on the new epoxy floor?+
Light foot traffic is typically safe after 24 hours. Vehicle traffic should wait a minimum of 72 hours — 5–7 days is ideal for full cure before parking hot tires, which can cause surface impressions in incompletely cured coating. We'll give you specific guidance based on the products used and the conditions at installation.
How do I maintain an epoxy floor?+
Epoxy floors are very low maintenance. Sweep or dust mop regularly, and mop with a dilute neutral-pH cleaner as needed. Avoid harsh degreasers, which can damage the top coat over time. For oil spills, clean up promptly with a shop towel — epoxy is non-porous but standing oil can stain over extended contact. We provide a complete care guide at project completion.
