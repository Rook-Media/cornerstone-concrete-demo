What's Included in Every Patio & Walkway Project

A concrete patio Nashville homeowners enjoy for decades is the result of careful planning, proper drainage, and the right finish choice. Here's our standard scope.

✓

Design Consultation

We discuss shape, size, finish options, and how the patio connects to your home and yard. We advise on what works best structurally, visually, and practically for your specific property.

✓

Grading & Drainage Planning

Water that pools near your home's foundation causes expensive problems. We grade every patio away from the structure and plan drainage solutions where needed — before a single form goes in.

✓

Wire Mesh or Rebar Reinforcement

Patio slabs are typically reinforced with welded wire mesh on chairs. For larger areas, tiered patios, or areas with suspected soil movement, we step up to rebar — discussed at estimate.

✓

Finish Options Included

Broom finish, exposed aggregate, salt finish, or stamped texture — we discuss all options at the estimate and provide samples so you can make an informed decision before committing.

✓

Edge Forms & Border Details

Clean edges and optional decorative borders define the patio and protect it from edge spalling over time. We form edges properly and offer integral borders in contrasting patterns or colors.

✓

Sealed & Ready

Every completed patio is sealed before we leave, protecting your investment from the first rain. We provide written care instructions and sealer re-application guidance.

How We Work

Our Patio Installation Process

Here's what to expect from a Cornerstone patio project — from the first site visit to your finished outdoor living space.

1

Free Estimate

We visit, discuss your vision, assess the space, and deliver a written quote covering scope, materials, and timeline within 24 hours.

2

Excavation & Base

We excavate, grade for proper drainage, and install compacted base material. Forms are set precisely before any concrete is ordered.

3

Pour & Finish

Concrete is placed, screeded, floated, and finished to your chosen texture. Steps, borders, and decorative elements are completed in the same pour where possible.

4

Cure & Walkthrough

Proper cure period followed, sealer applied, site cleaned. Final walkthrough with you to review the finished patio and answer any questions.

Why Cornerstone

Why Choose Cornerstone for Your Concrete Patio in Nashville

Outdoor Living That Holds Up to Nashville's Weather

Nashville sees everything — hot, humid summers, cold snaps, and spring rains that can drop several inches in a single storm. A concrete patio Nashville homeowners invest in needs to handle all of it without pooling water near the foundation, heaving in a cold snap, or cracking from UV exposure and thermal cycling. We design every patio with proper slope (a minimum of 1/4 inch per foot away from the structure), plan drainage where the grade works against us, and use mix designs and sealing practices suited to Middle Tennessee's climate. What looks like a simple slab pour is actually a detailed engineering and timing exercise when it's done right.

Concrete Outperforms Pavers for Low-Maintenance Outdoor Living

Pavers look beautiful immediately after installation, but they require ongoing maintenance that concrete does not. Individual pavers shift and settle, particularly in Nashville's clay soil where ground movement is common. Weeds and grass grow up between the joints, requiring regular attention. In a freeze-thaw event, individual pavers can pop loose and create tripping hazards. A properly installed concrete patio eliminates all of these maintenance concerns. The surface is monolithic — there are no joints for weeds to exploit and no individual pieces to reset. Re-sealing every few years is the primary maintenance task, and it's simple.

We Build for the Project in Front of Us, Not a Template

Some contractors show up with a standard approach and apply it to every job. We take time at the estimate visit to understand your specific yard — where the sun hits, where water runs, what the grade looks like, how you plan to use the space. A patio that's perfect for entertaining a crowd needs different proportions and different drainage planning than a simple back-step landing. We bring all of this thinking to your written quote so you understand exactly what we're building and why. Nashville homeowners who invest in outdoor concrete with Cornerstone consistently tell us the finished project is better than they imagined.

Questions

Patio & Walkway FAQ

How much does a concrete patio cost in Nashville?+
Concrete patio cost depends heavily on size, finish type, and any additional elements like steps or borders. Plain broom-finish patios typically run less than stamped or exposed aggregate options. We provide free, itemized written estimates so you know exactly what you're paying for — no guessing and no surprises at final invoice. Call or fill out our estimate form to schedule a site visit.
How long does a new concrete patio take to complete?+
Most residential patio installations take 1–2 days of active work, followed by 7 days of cure time before light use. Larger patios, tiered levels, or projects that include steps or retaining walls may take 2–3 days of active installation. We'll give you a realistic timeline in your written estimate and stick to it.
Can you pour a patio in sections to fit an irregular yard shape?+
Absolutely. We regularly work with irregular lot shapes, trees, existing landscaping, and challenging grade changes. We plan the form layout during the estimate visit and discuss how sections connect, where control joints go, and how the finished design will look. Pouring in sections is sometimes the right choice for large areas — it's all in the planning.
Concrete patio vs. pavers — which is better for Nashville?+
Both have merits, but concrete typically wins on long-term durability and maintenance in Middle Tennessee's clay-heavy soil. Pavers shift and settle over time as the ground moves, require periodic re-leveling and joint sand replacement, and can become weed magnets. Concrete is a monolithic surface — sealed, stable, and low maintenance. For most Nashville homeowners prioritizing long-term performance, concrete is the better investment.
