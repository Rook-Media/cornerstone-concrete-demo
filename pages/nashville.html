Local Work

Why Nashville Homeowners Choose Cornerstone

Nashville's diverse neighborhoods present a wide range of concrete challenges that we've spent 18 years navigating. Green Hills and Belle Meade properties often feature significant grade changes, mature trees with established root systems, and large-lot driveways that require careful drainage planning. We've completed dozens of projects in these neighborhoods, navigating existing landscaping and tight timelines while delivering the clean, long-lasting results these properties demand.

East Nashville's character-rich older homes frequently have driveways and walkways that haven't been updated in decades. We see a lot of repair and replacement work in this part of the city — concrete that was installed without proper reinforcement or base preparation, now showing the effects of Nashville's clay soil movement and years of freeze-thaw cycling. Our root-cause approach means we fix these problems correctly, not just cosmetically.

Germantown's urban density and tight lots create unique access and forming challenges that we're well-equipped to handle. We've worked in alley-accessed properties, narrow side yards, and historic structures where preserving existing hardscape while updating adjacent concrete requires precision and experience. Nashville's continued growth means we're also completing significant new-construction flatwork in areas like Nations and Wedgewood-Houston as the city expands into new neighborhoods. Whether your project is a straightforward driveway replacement or a complex multi-level outdoor space in one of Nashville's hillier zip codes, Cornerstone has the experience to do it right.

Questions

Nashville Concrete FAQ

Do Nashville concrete projects require permits?+
Most residential driveway and patio replacements within Nashville's Metro limits don't require permits when replacing like-for-like. Retaining walls over 4 feet, new curb cuts, and work in the public right-of-way typically do. We verify requirements for your specific address and handle all permitting on your behalf when required.
How quickly can you get to a Nashville property for an estimate?+
We typically schedule estimate visits within 2–3 business days of your request for properties in Nashville proper. During peak season (spring and summer), lead times may extend slightly. Call us directly for urgent situations — we accommodate time-sensitive requests when possible.
How long does a typical Nashville driveway or patio project take?+
Most residential Nashville concrete projects take 1–2 days of active work, followed by 7 days of cure time. Larger projects, multi-level patios, or retaining walls may take 3–5 days of work. We provide a specific project timeline in your written estimate and update you promptly if anything changes due to weather.
