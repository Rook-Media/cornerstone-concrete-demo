Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Straight answers before you commit to a single thing.

How long does concrete take to cure before I can use it? +
Concrete reaches about 70% of its full strength within 7 days and full cure at 28 days. For foot traffic, we typically recommend waiting 24–48 hours. For vehicle traffic on a driveway, we ask for a minimum of 7 days. We'll give you specific guidance based on your project, the mix design, and the weather at time of pour.
Do you offer color and texture options? +
Yes — we offer a wide range of integral color mixes, acid stains, and surface-applied color hardeners. For texture, stamped concrete can replicate brick, cobblestone, slate, flagstone, wood plank, and many other patterns. We'll bring sample boards to your estimate appointment so you can see exactly what the options look like in person.
What areas do you serve? +
We serve 16 cities across Middle Tennessee including Nashville, Brentwood, Franklin, Nolensville, Spring Hill, Thompson's Station, Columbia, Murfreesboro, Smyrna, La Vergne, Mt. Juliet, Lebanon, Hendersonville, Goodlettsville, Gallatin, and White House. If you're within 60 miles of downtown Nashville, give us a call.
How do you decide between repairing and replacing concrete? +
We evaluate the extent and cause of the damage during your free estimate. Surface cracks and minor spalling can often be repaired cost-effectively. When the slab has shifted, the subbase has failed, or damage covers more than 30–40% of the surface, replacement is usually the better long-term investment. We always give you an honest assessment — we won't push replacement if repair is the right answer.
